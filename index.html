About Me



I'm a PhD student at George Washington University doing research at NASA Goddard Space Flight Center. I study the most powerful explosions in the universe, known as gamma-ray bursts. They are brief flashes of gamma-rays known to occur as the result of the collapse of rapidly-rotating massive stars or neutron star mergers. I make predictions for next-generation gamma-ray burst observations that will be made by COSI, an upcoming gamma-ray space telescope, and study gamma-ray bursts observed by the Fermi Gamma-ray Burst Monitor.

Outside of research, I play harp, do a partnered social dance called Brazilian zouk, boulder, go for long walks, and care for my many houseplants.
